India is a vast and diverse country with 28 states and 8 union territories, each with its own culture, language, and traditions.

Rajasthan is the largest state by area, known for its desert landscape, forts, and palaces. Its capital is Jaipur, also called the Pink City. Uttar Pradesh is the most populous state, with cities like Lucknow and Varanasi.

Maharashtra is an economic powerhouse, home to Mumbai, the financial capital of India. Tamil Nadu in the south has a rich Dravidian cultural heritage, with Chennai as its capital.

West Bengal, with Kolkata as its capital, is known for its literary and artistic contributions. Kerala is famous for its backwaters and high literacy rate. Gujarat is known for its vibrant festivals and is the birthplace of Mahatma Gandhi.

Goa is the smallest state by area, known for its beaches and tourism. The northeastern states, such as Assam, Manipur, and Meghalaya, are known for their unique cultural identities and natural beauty.
