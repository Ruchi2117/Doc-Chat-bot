The primary colors are red, blue, and yellow. These are called primary colors because they cannot be created by mixing other colors together.

Secondary colors are created by mixing two primary colors:
- Mixing red and blue creates purple
- Mixing blue and yellow creates green
- Mixing red and yellow creates orange

Tertiary colors and other derived colors:
- Pink is created by mixing red and white
- Gray is created by mixing black and white
- Brown is created by mixing complementary colors or by mixing all three primary colors
- Peach is created by mixing red, yellow, and white

Complementary colors are pairs of colors that are opposite each other on the color wheel:
- Red and green are complementary colors
- Blue and orange are complementary colors
- Yellow and purple are complementary colors

Warm colors include red, orange, and yellow. These colors are associated with sunlight, fire, and heat.
Cool colors include blue, green, and purple. These colors are associated with water, ice, and shade.

Color values and properties:
- White is the presence of all colors of light combined
- Black is the absence of all colors or light
- Pink is a light tint of red, made lighter by adding white
- Pastels are any color mixed with white to create a softer, lighter version
